# Composite Operator Cover System v1

## Philosophy

Composite Operator covers should feel permanent, institutional, academic, and expensive. They should look designed by a publisher, not by a marketer.

The artwork carries the emotion. The typography carries the authority.

The signature test: the cover should look plausible between a CFA Institute publication, a University of Chicago Press economics book, and a hedge fund annual macro letter. If it feels like a finance YouTube thumbnail, start over.

## Page Architecture

Use portrait format, especially 2:3 or 6x9.

Divide the cover vertically:

| Zone | Target Height |
| --- | --- |
| Top breathing space | 12-15% |
| Title block | 25-30% |
| Subtitle block | 8-10% |
| Artwork field | 45-55% |
| Bottom breathing space | 5-10% |

Text owns the upper half. Artwork owns the lower half.

Never let artwork compete with the title. The title must sit in calm space, with the visual energy center of the artwork roughly 55-65% down the page.

## Publisher Imprint

Use a small institutional imprint at the top.

Preferred imprint:

`COMPOSITE OPERATOR RESEARCH DESK`

Style:

- Serif or small-cap serif.
- Small size.
- Generous tracking.
- Deep navy-black.
- No logo required.

## Primary Title

Preferred families:

- Trajan Pro
- Cormorant Garamond
- Canela
- Adobe Caslon Display
- EB Garamond

Fallback:

- Baskerville
- Garamond
- Georgia

Avoid:

- Sans serif
- Geometric fonts
- Modern fintech fonts
- Futura
- Montserrat
- Gotham

Use light to regular weight. Never rely on bold. Authority comes from scale, spacing, and restraint.

## Title Hierarchy

The standard hierarchy:

`THE`

Main title words:

`MACRO REGIME`

Supporting line:

`OPERATING MANUAL`

For pamphlets or field notes, adapt the same hierarchy:

`THE`

`FLOATING-RATE`

`FAULT LINE`

Supporting classification:

`PRIVATE CREDIT RISK EDUCATIONAL MATERIAL`

The main title should be 3-4x larger than subtitle text.

## Tracking

Tracking is central to the institutional feel.

Approximate rules:

- `THE`: +300 to +400
- Main title words: +50 to +100
- Supporting classification: +250 to +350
- Publisher imprint: +180 to +260

Increase spacing until it feels excessive, then stop.

## Font Sizes

For a 6x9 cover:

- Imprint: 8-11 pt
- `THE`: 22-28 pt
- Main title: 80-120 pt
- Supporting classification: 18-26 pt
- Subtitle: 14-18 pt italic

For responsive HTML, preserve these ratios rather than fixed points.

## Color System

Title:

- `#0A1324`
- `#111827`
- `#16202D`

Never use pure black. Pure black feels cheap.

Accent:

- `#B38A3A`
- `#C6A15B`

Use accent color only for hairlines, tiny ornaments, and restrained divider rules. Never use it for large title text.

## Subtitle

Subtitle style:

- Italic serif.
- Book weight.
- Centered.
- Narrower than title.
- Secondary, not promotional.

Example:

`SOFR, Private Credit, BDCs, and the Risk Curve`

## Divider Rules

Between title/classification and subtitle, use a thin centered divider.

Recommended structure:

- Hairline left rule.
- Tiny centered diamond or understated ornament.
- Hairline right rule.

Use restrained metallic gold. The divider must not become a logo.

## Spacing

Suggested rhythm:

`THE`

One line gap.

Main title.

Half-line gap.

Supporting classification.

One and a half line gap.

Divider.

One line gap.

Subtitle.

## Artwork

Artwork begins below the subtitle and may extend beneath the text as long as the upper field remains calm.

The artwork should be abstract, atmospheric, and print-worthy:

- Warm archival paper.
- Translucent planes.
- Mineral wash.
- Ink granulation.
- Copper or gold stress lines.
- Deep blue-black structural pressure.
- Teal or blue-green liquidity fields.

Avoid literal charts, candlesticks, logos, stock-photo finance imagery, and anything resembling a video thumbnail.

## Series Rule

For an entire library, keep typography identical and change only the artwork.

Examples:

- `THE MACRO REGIME OPERATING MANUAL`
- `THE PRIVATE CREDIT OPERATING MANUAL`
- `THE LIQUIDITY OPERATING MANUAL`
- `THE CAPITAL CYCLE OPERATING MANUAL`
- `THE MARKET REGIME OPERATING MANUAL`

Same font. Same hierarchy. Same spacing. Same navy title. Only the artwork changes.
